Feijoada

Feijoada is a typical food from Brazil, maybe the most famous of all. It's include a strong and unique flavour that isn't encoutered anywhere. Each region has your variance, but all of them becomes from the same recipe that I describe below.

Ingredients

  • 1x pig's ears cut in small pieces;
  • 2x pig's feet cut in small pieces;
  • 0,5kg of dried meat cut in small pieces;
  • 0,5kg of pig's rib cut in small pieces;
  • 0,5k of black bean
  • 2x tablespoons of soy oil;
  • 1x chopped onion;
  • 300g of pepperoni sausage in thick slices;
  • 300g of Paio sausage in thick slices;
  • 400g of boneless pig's rib
  • An orange
  • 2l of boiling water

Prepare

  1. The day before, put pig's ears and feet in a bowl and cover them with cold water. Change the water 4x during this time;
  2. In another bowl, put the dried meat and pig's rib and cover them with cold water to desalt. Change the water 4x;
  3. In the next day, put the beab in a bowl and cover with water for 1h;
  4. Drain the dried meat, the ears and rib and put them in a pressure cooker. Close the pot and cook for 15 minutes counted by start of the pressure. Remove from the fire, await all vaporwave exit. Drain and hold.
  5. In the same cookpot, heat the oil and do the onion become golden. Merge the sausage, paio, rib and saut√© for more 3 minutes. Merge the drained bean, the pre-cooked meat and the orange.
  6. Add 2x bacon bouillon cubes and the boiling water. Close the cookpot and wait 15 minutes starting from the pressure;
  7. Remove from the fire, wait the vapor exits and open the cookpot. Remove the orange and put again on the fire.
  8. Cook in the lower fire with the pot partially covered (without pressure) for more 30m or thicken the broth and the ingredients be cooked.
  9. Transfer to a dish and serve.
